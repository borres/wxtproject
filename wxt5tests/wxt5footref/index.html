Whenever you read some text relating to computers and the WWW1 World Wide Web, you find a lot of lingo2 a characteristic language of a particular group. Some examples are WYSIWYG3 what you see is what you get, AJAX4 Asynchronous JavaScript And XML and CAPTCHA5 Completely Automated Turing Test To Tell Computers and Humans Apart. More on Turing tests at The Alan Turing Home Page [1] or at Wikipedia , inspired from the works of Alan Turing [1] .

Of course this lingo is usefull for precision and ease of discussion. We need some precision to discuss the differences and similarities between for instance MVC6 Model View Controller as introduced by Trygve Reenskaug [2] and the more recently coined principle of SoC7 Separation of Concerns, Wikipedia has an introduction [3] .

On the other hand you sometimes get the feeling that the lingo serves the same purpose as one can suspect that latin did in medieval services; To mystify more than to clearify.

Personally I think we should try to apply the KISS8 Keep It Simple Stupid-principle, in most situations of life.
